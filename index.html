Helping Moms & Babies Thrive in Wartime Ukraine

As the war rages on in Ukraine, new mothers can face overwhelming stress in caring for their newborns. In partnership with the Ukrainian government, UNICEF is providing critical support to families through the Home Visiting Program. Trained nurses provide new mothers with parenting advice and health guidance, along with depression screening and emotional support. In Pavlohrad, eastern Ukraine, Yulia and other expectant mothers had to move to a bomb shelter when a local chemical plant caught fire after being damaged by shelling. A home nurse helped keep her calm during labor and continued to support her in the weeks after.

Extreme Weather in Pakistan Continues to Devastate Families

Over a year after catastrophic flooding in Pakistan, many are still struggling. The sweltering summer heat, exacerbated by the ongoing climate crisis, has made conditions even worse for families. Kamli, a mother of seven whose family was displaced to a temporary roadside camp, says, “We live with fear in our bodies, with the anxiety of not knowing when the next disaster will be.” UNICEF estimates there are still 8 million people, around half of whom are children, that continue to live without access to safe water in flood-affected areas. UNICEF teams are on the ground, distributing safe drinking water and hygiene kits, screening and treating children for severe acute malnutrition, providing psychosocial support and more.

Keeping Refugee Girls in School in Bangladesh

Today, nearly 1 million Rohingya refugees are living in Cox’s Bazar, Bangladesh after military-led violence in Myanmar displaced them from their homes. Families in the world’s largest refugee camp live in densely packed houses made of tin and cardboard. Children are often exposed to sexual violence and exploitation, child labor and other human rights violations. Girls like Roshida, who lost both of her parents at a young age, struggle to stay in school and are often forced into child marriage to survive — but education can be a lifeline. A UNICEF-hosted program is taking a holistic approach to help girls stay in school and reach their full potential. Roshida is now regularly attending class, recently passed her exams and looks forward to continuing her education.

Why Nikki and Gheed Joined UNICEF Clubs

Young people have the power and the passion to make the world a better place. As former UNICEF USA National Council members, Nikki and Gheed traveled to Washington D.C. to advocate for the inclusion of mental health services in foreign assistance. They share the stories that sparked their activism, the tangible impacts they made on their own communities and how UNICEF Clubs helped them deepen their impact.
